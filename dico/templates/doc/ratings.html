Rating Arguments

Rating arguments gives you and others the opportunity to weigh in on the quality of the arguments presented in support of or in opposition to an action. By rating arguments, you contribute to the quality of the debate around an action by informing others not only whether you support or oppose an action, but also what reasons you share for your vote.

You can rate arguments as:

  • Central - Fundamentally important to support for or opposition against this action.
  • Peripheral - Relevant but not fundamentally important.
  • Irrelevant - True, but not connected to this action.
  • Opinion - Others may reasonably disagree.
  • Misleading - Promotes an impression that is irrelevant, based on opinion or false.
  • False - Based on untrue statements.
